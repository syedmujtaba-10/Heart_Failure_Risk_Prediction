Causes of Heart Failure (HF)

Heart failure is a condition that occurs when the heart is not able to pump blood effectively to the rest of the body. There are several factors that can contribute to the development of heart failure, including:


Coronary artery disease: A condition where the arteries that supply blood to the heart become narrowed or blocked, reducing blood flow to the heart muscle.

High blood pressure: Over time, high blood pressure can cause the heart to work harder than it should, leading to heart failure.

Diabetes: People with diabetes are at higher risk for heart disease and therefore, heart failure.

Valve problems: Problems with heart valves can affect blood flow through the heart, leading to heart failure.

Cardiomyopathy: This is a condition where the heart muscle becomes weak and cannot pump blood effectively.

Congenital heart defects: Some people are born with structural abnormalities in their hearts that can eventually lead to heart failure.

For more information about heart failure and prevention, you can visit the American Heart Association's website at https://www.heart.org/en/health-topics/heart-failure
